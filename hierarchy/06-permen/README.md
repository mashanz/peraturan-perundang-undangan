# Permen - Peraturan Menteri (Ministerial Regulations)

## Metadata
- **Jenis:** Peraturan Menteri (Permen)
- **Hierarki:** Level 6  
- **Lembaga Pembentuk:** Menteri dan Pimpinan Lembaga Setingkat
- **Dasar Hukum:** UU No. 12 Tahun 2011 tentang Pembentukan Peraturan Perundang-undangan

## Function
Ministerial Regulations are issued by Ministers and heads of non-ministerial institutions to implement laws, government regulations, and presidential regulations within their respective sectors.

## Document Types by Ministry
Each ministry typically issues regulations on:
- **Organizational structure** - Internal ministry organization
- **Technical standards** - Sector-specific technical requirements
- **Service procedures** - Public service delivery mechanisms  
- **Licensing and permits** - Sectoral business licensing
- **Implementation guidelines** - Detailed implementation of laws/PP
- **Professional standards** - Industry/professional certification requirements

## Legal Status Categories
- **Berlaku** - Currently active and enforceable
- **Dicabut** - Formally revoked by newer regulations
- **Diubah** - Amended by newer regulations  
- **Diganti** - Completely replaced by newer regulations

## Integration with Legal Hierarchy
Ministerial regulations must align with and implement:
- **Level 3:** Laws (UU/Perppu) - Sectoral laws
- **Level 4:** Government Regulations (PP) - Implementation frameworks
- **Level 5:** Presidential Regulations (Perpres) - Executive policies

## Priority Expansion Sequence
1. **Ministry of Finance** - Tax, customs, financial regulations (highest public impact)
2. **Ministry of Home Affairs** - Regional government, population administration
3. **Ministry of Law and Human Rights** - Legal framework, immigration, corrections
4. **Ministry of Health** - Healthcare, pharmaceuticals, medical devices  
5. **Ministry of Industry** - Manufacturing standards, industrial development
